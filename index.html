1. Introduction

In recent years, as the impact of the pandemic has begun to wane, there has been a surge in leisure travel [1]. Unlike business travelers, many leisure travelers choose to fly an airline based on ticket prices, often opting to fly Low-Cost Carriers (LCCs) [1] [2]. While economy class seats on-board an LCC are not comfortable to begin with due to prioritizing maximizing seat count and minimizing costs, this discomfort becomes even more apparent on long-haul flights, flights over six hours long, which majority of leisure flyers are embarking on [3]. As such, passengers are starting to choose airlines based on comfort along with price [4]. Since this comfort is readily available on Full-Service Carriers (FSCs), it becomes easier to choose them over LCCs. Therefore, there is a need to re-design economy class seats for LCCs to eliminate discomfort, especially on long-haul flights, to improve passenger experience and introduce a new competitive factor for LCCs.



2. Project Clarification

2.1. Background

An LCC, contrary to popular belief, focuses on minimizing operating costs rather than offering low ticket prices; it is the opposite to an FSC. For example, Singapore Airlines is an FSC while Scoot is an LCC. There are three main ways through which an LCC minimizes operational costs:
1) Using fewer aircraft models
2) Having single cabin configurations
3) Choosing to operate out of secondary airports

Their ticket pricing mechanism, which will not be discussed in this report, revolves around offering minimum amenities needed for transporting a person from point A to point B as a part of the ticket price while imposing additional charges for incentives such as in-flight meals and check-in baggage. With lower operating costs, cost savings can be passed on to the traveler, encouraging more passengers to travel [5].
